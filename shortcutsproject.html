iOS Shortcuts Redesign


Redesigning the iOS Shortcuts app to eliminate design inconsistencies and reduce friction in the user's workflow. Aimed to provide more user clarity and lower the learning curve for beginner users.

UX Designer

Grad School Project

2 months (2024)

Figma

Project Overview


Context

Final project for our HCID 570 class, which explored design patterns & contexts normally found in everyday world. My team chose to revamp the iOS Shortcuts app, citing its unintuitive user experience and lack of in-app support as potential problems to address.

Brief

Simplify the process of creating shortcuts, shorten the learning curve, and improve usability.

Design Decisions

Q: How to improve consistency in design elements?

A: Refine element design to align with Apple's iPhone design system

Apple users are familiar with how the system responds to their gestures and inputs. Shortcuts's design elements go against these expectations, which leads to more guesswork and frustration as the user moves through the app. To repair this disconnect and bridge the gap between mental model and system feedback, I redesigned buttons and modals within Shortcuts to more closely resembles components in Apple's existing design system.

Q: How to reduce user confusion while learning the app?

A: Add visual cues, remove distractions, and break up the learning curve into smaller steps from beginner to advanced features

As they learn to use Shortcuts, beginners often become overwhelmed by the app's massive collection of usable actions and lack of guidance, which leads to high drop-off rates. By limiting the user's premature exposure to advanced features, I aimed to created a more structured onboarding experience for beginners that guides them through the shortcut creation process and allows them to get used to the basics first.

Takeaways

This project was both challenging and exciting to work on. While I'm pleased with what I could produce in such a short amount of run time, I had to prioritize the most impactful elements, leaving many areas of improvement unaddressed. Some action items I'd like to keep working on include:

  • Redesign the in-app Help section
  • Conduct additional user testing to refine the revamped Homepage and creation process
